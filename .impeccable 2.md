## Design Context

### Users

Nantucket Event Co. owners and administrators build schedules, assign crews and
vehicles, and prepare event pack lists. Field staff use the app on phones,
often outdoors or while moving quickly, to understand exactly where to go,
when to arrive, what to bring, and what to do. Staff-facing information must be
unmistakable at a glance and resilient to rushed or low-attention use.

### Brand Personality

Refined, dependable, and operationally clear. The interface should feel like
Nantucket Event Co.: established and polished, but never precious or difficult
to operate. It should create confidence and calm under time pressure.

### Aesthetic Direction

Preserve the existing light editorial identity: warm paper-toned surfaces,
Nantucket navy, restrained coral accents, the company logo, Instrument Sans
for operational UI, and Newsreader for selective display moments. The product
is mobile-first, with an iOS Calendar-like sense of familiarity. Avoid generic
SaaS gradients, dark dashboards, excessive decoration, dense controls, and
visual effects that compete with schedule information.

### Design Principles

1. Make the next required action and the day’s operational details immediately
   obvious, especially for field staff.
2. Favor large readable type, generous touch targets, plain language, and
   strong hierarchy over compactness.
3. Adapt controls deliberately for phones; never permit horizontal overflow,
   accidental browser zoom, obscured content, or inaccessible actions.
4. Keep owner/admin power available without making routine views feel crowded.
5. Preserve the established Nantucket visual system and make refinements
   surgical rather than redesigning working interfaces.
